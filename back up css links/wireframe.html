The history of Telifox Corporation starts in the 1800s when the English inventor Von Pier purchased a large farm in the Midwest state of Ohio. Von Pier accumulated enough financial resources through farming profits to invest in a local Pharmacy just 20 minutes north of modern-day Dayton, Ohio. The Pharmacy was the first in Ohio to utilize the local herbs and plants to cure common sicknesses. The Pharmacy eventually was named Red Fox Pharmacies in which resulted in the creation of a Red Fox Pharmacy Chain. The Red Fox Pharmacy went on to dominate the local medical market in Dayton, Ohio by 1850. The company developed various brand name medical products and became one of the biggest Pharmacies in the USA by the 1900s. Von Pier had 12 children with three wives and he eventual passed on his Pharmacy legacy to his Eldest Son Michael Pier in 1847. Michael brought the Pharmacy to new heights by signing a government contract developing cures for the deadliest viruses. This success launched the Red Fox Pharmacy on the world market.
